Day 1 · Containers A1 + B1
Coconut Adobo Chicken Stew
Whole chicken, broken down · Stew-style
Protein
  • Whole chicken (broken down: thighs, drumsticks, breast quarters)
Vegetables
  • Broccoli
  • Carrots (chunked)
  • Pechay / bok choy
  • Ginger (sliced)
  • Red onion (quartered)
Seasoning
  • Coconut aminos
  • Coconut vinegar
  • Garlic (5-6 cloves, crushed)
  • Bay leaves (2-3)
  • Peppercorns (whole, cracked)
  • Coconut oil
Kimchi
  • Served on the side, not cooked in
Chef's Guidance

Adobo-style braise using coconut aminos instead of soy. Brown the chicken pieces skin-side down in coconut oil first; get real color on the skin before anything else goes in.

Build the braise: garlic, onion, peppercorns, bay leaves, generous pour of coconut aminos, splash of vinegar. Ratio is roughly 3 parts aminos to 1 part vinegar; adjust to taste. Liquid should come about halfway up the chicken, not cover it.

Simmer low and slow with lid on until chicken is falling-off-the-bone tender. Add carrots about halfway through. Broccoli and pechay go in during the last 5-8 minutes only; they should still have color and bite.

Ginger slices stay in the broth the entire time. The broth should reduce to something thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Optional lemon squeeze at the end to finish.

Day 2 · Containers A2 + B2
Garlic-Ginger Pork with Coconut Oil Veg
Pork sliced thin · Seared hot and fast
⚠ Last order the pork came out tough and overdone. Sear hot and fast; 2-3 minutes per side max. Pull off heat and let rest. Better slightly pink inside than dry.
Protein
  • ~750g pork loin or shoulder, sliced thin against the grain (~1cm medallions or strips)
Vegetables
  • Cabbage (rough chopped)
  • Bell pepper (strips)
  • Zucchini (half-moons)
  • Red onion (sliced)
Seasoning
  • Garlic (4-5 cloves, minced)
  • Ginger (minced, small amount)
  • Coconut aminos
  • Coconut oil
  • Black pepper (coarse)
  • Lemon juice to finish
Chef's Guidance

Marinate sliced pork at least 30 minutes: coconut aminos + garlic + ginger + cracked pepper.

Sear in a hot pan with coconut oil. Get a caramel color from the aminos sugars. Don't crowd the pan; do batches if needed. Pull each batch when just done.

Cook veg separately in the same pan after the pork. Coconut oil, onion first, then bell pepper and cabbage, zucchini last (it goes mushy fast). Everything should still have crunch.

Hit the veg with a little coconut aminos and a squeeze of lemon at the end. Layer pork and veg in the container separately; keeps textures distinct when reheating.

Day 3 · Containers A3 + B3
Lemon-Annatto Chicken (Inasal Style)
Chicken cuts · Grilled or pan-seared
Protein
  • 1kg chicken breast + thigh cuts
Vegetables
  • Pechay / bok choy
  • Carrots (julienned or thin coins)
  • Red onion (rings)
Seasoning
  • Lemon juice (fresh)
  • Garlic (generous)
  • Ginger (small amount)
  • Annatto/atsuete seeds
  • Coconut oil
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
Chef's Guidance

Inspired by Bacolod chicken inasal. Make the marinade: lemon juice, minced garlic, minced ginger, black pepper, and annatto oil (steep atsuete seeds in warm coconut oil 10-15 minutes for the orange color, then strain).

Marinate chicken at least 1 hour; overnight in fridge is better if timing allows.

Grill or pan-sear in coconut oil. Baste with leftover marinade while cooking. Thighs handle more heat; breast pieces need attention so they don't dry out. Chef should use her judgment on doneness; juicy inside with charred edges is the goal.

Slice before putting in containers so it reheats evenly. Sauté pechay and carrots quickly in coconut oil with garlic; keep it simple. Onion rings can go raw or lightly charred. Lemon wedge in each container for squeezing at mealtime.

Day 4 · Containers A4 + B4
Kimchi-Ginger Pork Stew
Remaining pork, cubed · Slow-simmered
⚠ Stew pork is more forgiving than seared, but still shouldn't be boiled to death. Fork-tender, not dry threads. If using loin, keep cubes larger so they hold moisture.
Protein
  • ~750g pork, cut into bite-sized cubes
Vegetables
  • Broccoli
  • Cabbage (chopped)
  • Zucchini (chunked)
  • Ginger (sliced)
  • Red onion (diced)
Seasoning
  • Coconut aminos
  • Coconut oil
  • Garlic (crushed)
  • Black pepper
  • Kimchi (stirred in at the end)
Chef's Guidance

Brown the pork cubes in coconut oil first; build fond on the bottom of the pot. Don't skip this step; it's where the flavor comes from. Remove pork, sauté onion, garlic, ginger slices in the same pot.

Deglaze with coconut aminos and a splash of water or bone broth if available. Return pork, add enough liquid to almost cover, simmer. Low heat, lid on, 30-40 minutes or until fork-tender.

Add cabbage and broccoli in the last 10 minutes. Zucchini in the last 5. Kimchi goes in at the very end, off heat; stir through so it warms but doesn't cook down and lose its tang.

If chef has tamarind paste or powder available, a small amount in the broth gives it a sinigang-style sour backbone that pairs well with the kimchi. Optional; up to her.

Day 5 · Fresh Cook · No Container
Day 5 Eggs
Eggs + remaining veg + whatever's left · 10 minutes to cook
Ingredients
  • 4-6 eggs per person
  • Remaining raw veg (pechay, carrots, onion, etc.)
  • Coconut oil
  • Garlic
  • Black pepper
  • Lemon
  • Kimchi if any remains
Three Options

Option A: Egg Stir-Fry

Scramble eggs in coconut oil with garlic, toss in whatever veg is left, black pepper and lemon squeeze. Kimchi on the side.

Option B: Fried Eggs Over Garlic Veg

Sauté remaining veg in coconut oil with sliced garlic until just wilted. Plate, put fried eggs on top (runny yolk preferred). Black pepper, lemon.

Option C: Egg Drop Soup

If there's leftover broth from the stews, heat it up, beat eggs into it for a quick egg-drop with whatever veg scraps are around. Ginger and garlic base.

Bananas and fruit fill snack gaps on days 4 and 5.

Quick Reference for Chef

  • All cooking in coconut oil; no vegetable oil, no soy sauce
  • The dark sauce is coconut aminos; use wherever she'd normally use soy
  • Garlic and black pepper go heavy on everything; nothing should taste plain
  • Pork: don't overcook. Hot and fast for sliced (Meal 2). Low and slow for cubed stew (Meal 4). Last time it was tough.
  • Veg goes in last in every recipe; should still have color and texture
  • Kimchi is a side or stirred in at the end, never cooked into the braise
  • She has full latitude on seasoning ratios, timing, and finishing touches; these are frameworks, not scripts
  • All 8 containers filled and refrigerated on prep day; label Day 1 through Day 4
  • Leave ~25% of raw veg unprepped in fridge for Day 5 egg cooking

Fresh cook from remaining ingredients

Sorting Instructions for Chef
Step-by-step container filling on prep day
1

Label all 8 containers before filling

A1, A2, A3, A4 for Person 1. B1, B2, B3, B4 for Person 2. Use tape or marker. Day number on each lid.

2

Set aside ~25% of raw vegetables

Before cooking anything, pull aside a portion of pechay, carrots, onion, and any other veg for Day 5 fresh cooking. Store raw in a separate bag or container in the fridge.

3

Cook all four meals

All meat gets cooked on prep day. Follow the recipe order: Meal 1 (chicken stew), Meal 2 (pork seared), Meal 3 (inasal chicken), Meal 4 (pork stew). Vegetables go in last for each meal.

4

Fill containers while food is warm

Split each meal evenly between the A and B container for that day. For Meal 2 (seared pork): layer pork on one side, veg on the other; don't mix. For stews (Meals 1 & 4): include enough broth to keep meat moist during reheating.

5

Add sides

Kimchi gets a small portion in Day 1 and Day 4 containers. If containers have a divider or small compartment, use it. If not, kimchi in a small separate container or bag is fine. Lemon wedge goes in Day 3 containers.

6

Cool and refrigerate immediately

Let containers cool to room temperature (30-45 min max), then refrigerate. Don't stack while hot; condensation makes lids pop. Once cool, stack and organize by day in the fridge.

7

Rotation in the fridge

Day 1 and Day 2 containers in front (eat first). Day 3 and Day 4 containers behind. Protein rotation: chicken → pork → chicken → pork. No same protein two days in a row.
